1.3. Trigonometry#

1.3.1. Pythagorean indentity:#

(1.5)#\[ \sin^2 x+\cos^2 x=1 \]

1.3.2. Sine and cosine additive identities#

(1.6)#\[ \sin(x+y)=\sin x\cos y+\cos x\sin y \]
(1.7)#\[ \cos(x+y)=\cos x\cos y-\sin x\sin y \]

1.3.3. Double angle formulas:#

(1.8)#\[ \sin(2x)=2\sin x\cos x \]
(1.9)#\[ \cos(2x)=\cos^2 x-\sin^2 x=2cos^2 x-1=1-2\sin^2x \]

1.3.4. Half angle formulas:#

(1.10)#\[ \sin(\frac{x}{2})=\pm \sqrt{\frac{1-\cos x}{2}} \]
(1.11)#\[ \cos(\frac{x}{2})=\pm \sqrt{\frac{1+\cos x}{2}} \]

1.3.5. Complex numbers#

The imaginary unit, \(i\) is defined as: $\( i=\sqrt{-1} \)$ (eq:Complex_i)

A complex number is and expression of the form:

(1.12)#\[ z=x+iy \]

The distance from origo, the the point (x,y) in the complex plane is called the modulus and is defined as: $\( |z|=\sqrt{x^2+y^2} \)$ (eq:Complex_modulus)

The norm of a complex number (equivalent of the vector length in the complex plane) is: $\( |z|^2=x^2+y^2 \)$ (eq:Complex_norm_squared)

The modulus of a product of two complex numbers \(z\) and \(w\) is: $\( |zw|=|z||w| \)$ (eq:Complex_modulus_product)

The modulus of the sum of two complex numbers, also called “The triangle inequality” is defined: $\( |z+w|<|z|+|w| \)$ (eq:Complex_triangle_identity)

1.3.6. The Euler formula and modifications#

1.3.6.1. Eulers identity#

(1.13)#\[ e^{i\pi}+1=0 \]

1.3.6.2. Eulers formula#

(1.14)#\[ e^{ix}=\cos x+i\sin x \]

1.3.6.3. Cosine exponential form#

(1.15)#\[ \frac{e^{ix}+e^{-ix}}{2}=\cos x \]

1.3.6.4. Sine exponential form#

(1.16)#\[ \frac{e^{ix}-e^{-ix}}{2i}=\sin x \]

1.3.6.5. Tangent exponential form#

(1.17)#\[ \frac{e^{ix}-e^{-ix}}{i(e^{ix}+e^{-1x})}=\tan x \]

1.3.6.6. Complex exponential#

(1.18)#\[ e^{x+iy}=e^x(\cos y+i\sin y) \]

1.3.6.7. de Moivre’s theorem#

(1.19)#\[ (\cos x+i\sin x)^n=\cos nx+i\sin nx \]

You can read more about trigonometry in Adams and Essex [AE18], chapter P.6.
